Organization

Topic

Choose a topic for the X-athon.

This acts as a focus for selecting resources for description, and for the set of related entities used to describe and access them.

These entities and relationships can be identified in advance and included in a base r-ball for the X-athon.

The topic should support a sufficient range of works, expressions, and manifestations to give participants a choice of simplicity, complexity, familiarity, or specific metadata interest.

Many X-athons have used the works of a single person as a topic. The original "jane-athons" focused on the works of Jane Austen.

Scope

Consider restricting the scope of selected resources to avoid known issues, such as aggregations, or other complexities that may distract or take too much time to explore.

Venue

Most attendees at an X-athon will benefit from working in groups of 2 to 6, with a "team leader" for groups of 4 and up.

Some attendees will prefer to work on their own.

Tables and seating sould be arranged to allow this to happen.

Consider assigning tables or groups to specific aspects of RDA or RIMMF if there is a large number of attendees. Aspects should should reflect the topic and scope chosen for the X-athon. Examples of aspects in which a group may be interested include audio-visual resources, translations, and digitized resources.

Other venue facilities to consider include:

  • Sockets for laptop power supplies.
  • Wi-fi.
  • Projection facilities for presentations.
  • Catering.

Preparation

Base r-ball

Consider preparing a base r-ball in advance.

This acts as a "starter" set of RIMMF data for each attendee to use.

The entities included in the r-ball should reflect the topic chosen for the X-athon.

For example, for a "person" topic, a typical base r-ball for identifies and briefly describes relevant Work, Expression, and Person entities.

For further information, see How to prepare a base r-ball.

Sample resources

Consider selecting a set of sample resources for attendees to catalogue during the X-athon.

The resources should reflect the topic and scope chosen for the X-athon.

Consider selecting a mix of resources that have a MARC 21 record that is available for import into RIMMF, and those that require cataloguing from scratch:

  • Imported MARC 21 data is FRBRized, so the set of entities and relationships required for an RDA description is immediately visible.
  • Identifying, describing, and relating RDA entities from scratch, in a pure RDA workflow, reveals the context and functionality of the RDA guidance and instructions.

Resources can be real items, or images or transcriptions of the sources of manifestation statements such as title pages.

Consider encouraging attendees to bring their own resources.

For further information, see Bringing examples of resources to the X-athon

Access to RDA Toolkit

A special free &auot;trial" subscription to RDA Toolkit is available to ensure that X-athon attendees can consult the RDA guidance and instructions while using RIMMF.

Contact ALA Digital Reference to request access and for further information.
